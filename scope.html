10 EBI design passes — what changed and why▾
EBI 01 · Open on the truth
Keep the doomscroll cold-open.
Considered opening on the ring gesture and flashing back. Rejected — too on-the-nose. The doomscroll earns the interruption. No title card. Trust the image.
EBI 02 · Make the interruption tactile
Hand-touches-ring, not CG halo.
Original brief implied the pulse. Better: a visible tell — the other hand touches the ring instinctively. Physical, not mystical. No visual FX on the ring itself.
EBI 03 · Earn "three minutes later"
Stay with the doomscroller through daylight.
The critique named this shot specifically. Same person, same bed, still there — and then the morning after. No cast swap in Act 1. The credible delta is one person, one arc.
EBI 04 · Cast for vibe
Plurality with personal style.
Age, body, skin, style plurality — but every cast member has a specific taste. Not "diverse extras." The 64-year-old is cool. The teen is cool. Different codes.
EBI 05 · Phones are partners
Show intentional phone use.
Every original shot showed the phone going away. Better: include Shot 05 — checking the weather. The ring changes the relationship. It doesn't demonize the device.
EBI 06 · Compress to 3 acts
Interruption. Habitat. Loop.
Original 12-shots-at-7s-each read as an ambient reel. Reorganized as Interruption (0–30) · Habitat (30–60) · Loop (60–90). Hits harder. Easier to cut down to 30s.
EBI 07 · Score the ambient, name it
Low frequencies, no bells.
Reference: Nils Frahm-adjacent, Nick Hakim, textural Bon Iver. Warmth in the low end. No singing bowls. No breath tracks. No handpan.
EBI 08 · One line at the end
"The off-switch you wear."
Pulled from the investor overview. One line only. Specs (21-day battery, screen-free, titanium) live in the 30s cut-downs. The film delivers the promise, not the spec sheet.
EBI 09 · Give the ring a hero moment
Slow-mo macro in Act 2.
Shot 10 is the reward — 3 seconds, titanium texture, the pulse firing. Product porn, but earned by the emotional runway before it.
EBI 10 · Close where we opened
The bedroom, at 8:47pm.
End on a lock screen reading 8:47pm. Same room, different time — the customer chose the evening this time. Closes the loop the doomscroller opened.
10 more EBI passes · round 2 · script-level refinements▾
EBI 11 · Kill the voiceover impulse
Text-on-screen or nothing.
No narrator. Voiceovers age the film and read as ad-agency default. If we need words, place them as clean type-cards. The images earn the message.
EBI 12 · Name the enemy, once
"85% of us can't switch off."
Somewhere in Act 2 — a single type-card, 2 seconds. From the investor overview. The film has an antagonist: overstimulation. Say it once. Never again.
EBI 13 · Beat construction
Hook → tension → interruption → relief → habitat → loop.
Six emotional beats. Storyboard as-is has them but out of order. Resequence: doom (hook) → pulse (tension) → phone-down (interruption) → present bed (relief) → morning coffee (habitat) → the loop.
EBI 14 · Casting granularity
Write six casting sheets, not five.
Doomscroller · Designer · Kitchen dad · Subway friends · Hero-shot hand · Closer. Each with a 2-line character brief. Not "diverse person 30s" — a specific human with a specific taste.
EBI 15 · The ring gets its verb
"Pulls" — the ring pulls you out.
From the investor overview: "pulls the wearer out of autopilot." Every beat where the ring fires, the language and gesture are the same verb: PULL. Consistent grammar of the product.
EBI 16 · Sound-design ledger
Three cues, three registers.
1. Low sub-bass hum for the doomscroll (physical) · 2. A single soft haptic-cadence tick when the ring pulses (mechanical, not chime) · 3. Room-tone for the after (breath, distant traffic). No music in Act 1. Music enters at Act 2 shot 06.
EBI 17 · Cut down the cut-downs
Three 30-second variants, one 6-second bump.
30s A: Act 1 only. 30s B: Act 2 hero moment + tagline. 30s C: montage + tagline. 6s bump: the phone going face-down. Every social spec pre-planned. Nothing recut in a rush.
EBI 18 · Ring on the hero shot needs a body
Not floating in white space.
The 3-second macro (Shot 10) needs to be attached to a person doing something — cooking, typing, holding a coffee cup. Product porn only works if the ring belongs to a life.
EBI 19 · Type-card grammar
One weight. One case. One mono for utility.
Any type on screen matches the brand deck's type stack: TT Norms Pro light for headlines · DM Mono for utility · Playfair italic for the single verb we want to land. No sub-titles in mixed case, no fancy kerning. Discipline reads.
EBI 20 · End frame, not end card
The last image is the tagline.
Consider closing on the phone-down shot instead of a corporate outro card. Overlay: "Pulse. The off-switch you wear." · fade. The last frame is the argument.
